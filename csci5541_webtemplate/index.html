Abstract

This project explores how different Large Language Models (LLMs) respond to the Big Five Inventory Short Form (BFI-2-S) when prompted with synthetic demographic profiles. By collecting over 64 000 responses across four open-source LLMs and 540 profiles, we uncover stable, model-specific personality signatures with implications for fairness and personalization.

Methodology

Synthetic Profile Generation

  • Age (6): 18–25, 26–35, 36–45, 46–55, 56–65, 66+ years
  • Gender (3): Male, Female, Non-binary
  • Location (6): USA, Germany, India, Brazil, Japan, Australia
  • Education (5): High School, Associate’s, Bachelor’s, Master’s, Doctorate

Example: “I am a 36–45 year-old non-binary with a master’s degree living in India.”

Prompt Design

Each profile + BFI-2-S item is sent as:

  1. system message: You are a helpful assistant completing a Big Five personality survey.
  2. user message: demographics sentence + Answer with a single number: 1 = strongly disagree … 5 = strongly agree. No extra text. + backticked BFI statement

Conclusion & Future Work

  • Bias: Models encode demographic stereotypes.
  • Control: Demographic prompts steer tone and style.
  • Next steps: Human-baseline comparisons, prompt refinement, bias mitigation.
